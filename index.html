VR headset + Haptic device research

VIRTUAL REALITY

RESEARCH

UCL

I completed my second-year internship at Enseirb-Matmeca at UCL (University College London) under the supervision of Professor Anthony Steed, head of the VECG (Virtual Environments and Computer Graphics) research group. My project focused on developing a 3D simulation that integrated a virtual reality headset and a haptic device.

Oculus Rift + Moog HapticMaster setup in UCL's VR lab.

Professor Steed's idea was to create a simulation using two VR peripherals: the Oculus Rift headset and the Moog HapticMaster device. The Oculus Rift immerses the user in a 3D virtual environment using dual screens and optical lenses, along with various sensors to naturally translate the user's head movements onto the screen.

The haptic device, on the other hand, allows the user to manipulate objects in three dimensions through the sense of touch. The HapticMaster features a force-feedback system that provides the user with a tactile sensation when interacting with the virtual environment.

University College London.

Oculus Rift and Moog HapticMaster.

The simulation allows the user to move a red cube within a 3D space and interact with the virtual environment's walls. A high-gravity zone, represented by a blue square on the floor, exerts a downward force on the user's arm. A green cube serves as a damping zone where the user's movements are slowed. The Oculus Rift's high responsiveness and the HapticMaster's mechanical power (up to 50,000 Newtons of rigidity) make the experience exceptionally immersive.

BillAR - Augmented Reality Pool Game

AUGMENTED REALITY

GAME DEV

BillAR is student project combining a Microsoft Kinect for object movement detection and a video-projector used to display the UI of the game on a physical board.

I completed the BillAR project with a team of students and in collaboration with the INRIA laboratory in Bordeaux during my third year at Enseirb-Matmeca. The system was developed using PapARt ('Paper Augmented Reality Toolkit'), a Processing library created by Jérémy Laviole, a researcher at INRIA.

The concept behind BillAR is to create an augmented reality game that combines a pool table, a video projector, and a motion detection system (a Microsoft Kinect in our case). The game interface is projected directly onto the pool table via the video projector. The user can physically interact with it through touch, thanks to the motion detection system.

BillAR concept.

We invented several mini-games that take advantage of the possibilities offered by Augmented Reality. In Snake mode, the user plays with a countdown timer and aims to place the game ball (red in the diagram) in as many zones as possible within the allotted time. The zones appear one after the other, similar to the classic Snake game. Conversely, in Golf mode, all zones are present on the board from the start, and the player must place the red ball in each zone as quickly as possible.

Atos IT Challenge - 1st Prize

ENGINEERING

MOBILE DEV

Fellow students from Enseirb-Matmeca and I won the 2014 edition of the international student competition, Atos IT Challenge. The challenge was to develop an original mobile application to enhance the experience of visitors to the 2016 Rio Olympics.

The Idea

Our application idea, codenamed Olympic Smart Planner, is an intelligent assistant that automatically generates a schedule for the user's stay at the Rio Olympics. To suggest relevant events, the application uses the user's interests in various categories such as Sports, Culture, Restaurants, and Olympic Nations. This schedule is entirely customizable, allowing users to replace suggested events with other options. Additionally, users have access to a map of Rio de Janeiro displaying events from their schedule, providing GPS-guided navigation.

Olympic Smart Planner 2.0

The application developed during the IT Challenge was a demonstration prototype. During our internship at Atos, we took the opportunity to rethink the application's architecture. We emphasized the security of the network protocols used, the reliability of the server's web services, and the performance of the event and tourist location database. Simultaneously, we conducted a comprehensive redesign of the application to create a compelling product.
